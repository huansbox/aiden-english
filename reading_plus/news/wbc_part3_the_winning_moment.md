# The Winning Moment

**What happened in the last inning that made the players cry?**

1 It was the tenth inning. The score was tied, 4-4. Chinese Taipei needed one more run to win.

2 Chen got on base. He was the team captain. A pitch hit him in the first game of the tournament, and he missed two games. But now he was back, right when his team needed him most.

3 Chiang came up to bat. He did not swing hard. Instead, he tapped the ball softly down the first-base line. It was a bunt. The ball rolled slowly on the ground. Chen ran from third base as fast as he could. He touched home plate just in time! Chinese Taipei was ahead, 5-4!

4 Now they had to stop Korea from scoring. Korea tried hard. One runner tried to score from third base. Wu grabbed a ground ball and threw it home. The catcher, Chiang, caught it and tagged the runner. He was out! Everyone held their breath — was it really out? The umpire checked the replay. Out!

5 The game was over. Chinese Taipei won, 5-4! The players ran to each other and hugged. Some of them fell to the ground. Tears ran down their faces.

6 "At the end of the game, we played as one," said the manager. "And finally we got the win."

7 Chen looked up at the crowd and smiled. "Today," he said, "the Tokyo Dome became our home."
