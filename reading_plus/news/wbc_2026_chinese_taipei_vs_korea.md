# Instant Classic: Chinese Taipei stays alive with wild extra-inning win over Korea

**Source:** MLB.com
**Author:** Michael Clair
**Date:** March 8, 2026

---

More than 40,000 fans filled Tokyo Dome at noon on Sunday, predominantly from Taiwan, as Chinese Taipei's cheerleaders danced atop the dugouts while the band provided musical accompaniment. The contest delivered a dramatic conclusion when Chinese Taipei emerging with an incredible 5-4 victory in extra innings, with players walking off the field in tears.

Team captain Chieh-Hsien Chen declared, "I think today the Tokyo Dome became our home stadium."

This matchup held enormous significance. Chinese Taipei had never previously defeated Korea in WBC history, and the victory kept their advancement hopes alive after beginning the tournament winless. A Korean victory would have positioned them for a win-and-advance scenario Monday against Australia.

The game featured an intense back-and-forth sequence of home runs. Starting pitcher Hyun Jin Ryu, making his first national team appearance since 2008, allowed Yu Chang's solo shot in the first inning. Ruey-Guei Gu Lin matched this through four innings for Chinese Taipei.

Tsung-Che Cheng's solo home run in the sixth gave Chinese Taipei a 2-1 lead. Do-Yeong Kim responded with a two-run blast, knotting the game at 3. Stuart Fairchild's two-run shot in the eighth put Chinese Taipei ahead 4-3.

In the bottom eighth, Do-Yeong Kim's double tied the game again, forcing extra innings.

The decisive moment came in the tenth when Kun-Yu Chiang's bunt down the first-base line plated Chen from third. Chen, the 2024 Premier12 MVP, had been sidelined after being hit by a pitch in the tournament's first game.

Chinese Taipei then held Korea scoreless in the bottom half. A critical defensive play occurred when Nien-Ting Wu fielded a grounder and threw home to Shao-Hung Chiang to nail Ju Won Kim trying to tie the game. The challenged play was upheld.

Manager Hao-Jiu Tseng reflected: "At the end of the game, we played as one united, and finally we got the win."

Stuart Fairchild, whose grand slam eliminated the Czechs Saturday, noted the tournament's unique intensity: "What makes this tournament different is it feels like there's a completely different level of passion with players playing for their home countries."
